About Me

I am a member of the CSST Group at NAOC. My main concerns are the structures of the Milky Way and the nearby galaxies. I have studied the 3D density profile of the Milky Way disk with LAMOST. More recently I am working on the substructures such as the stellar streams with Gaia, LAMOST, DESI and S5.

Main Interests:

  • Stellar Streams
  • Disk/Halo profile
  • Nearby Galaxies
  • Large Survey Data Analysis

Selected Publications

2024 - MNRAS

Title: "Mapping the Milky Way with LAMOST - IV. The large Galactic disc extending to 35 kpc"
Authors: Hao Tian, Chao Liu, et al.
Summary: We use M giant stars to trace the disk density profile, which are selected from LAMOST spectroscopic survey. We found the thin disk can extend to 35 kpc from the Galactic center as showed in the right figure. Meanwhile, we found two break-points at R=14 and 20 kpc from the 3D density profile versus the Galactocentric radius R, which are represented by the vertical dashed lines. The scale lengths are 1.865, 2.428 and 5.950 kpc from inner to outer.

2024 - ApJ

Title: "A New Tidal Stream Discovered in Gaia DR3"
Authors: Hao Tian, et al.
Summary: With Gaia DR3, we discovered a new tidal stream, with length around 80 degree and with 1.7 degree as showed in the right figure. With help of RR Lyrae stars, we obtain the distance variance along the stream. Combining the spectra from LAMOST of a few giant stars, we studied the orbits of those stars and found the new stream is associated with the merging event Sequoia.
